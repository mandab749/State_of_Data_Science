Interesting Findings

Language Recommendations

We were very interested to see what the language recommendations were from those with many years of experience. It was no surprise that Python, R, and SQL were the top languages

Age & Country

One of the things we noticed was that the United States has more "experienced" data scientists. However, there seems to be quite a gap in the younger age bracket for US data scientists when compared to India. In a nutshell, we need more data scientists in the US (or more of them to answer the survey)

Gender & Coding

Another part that we noted was the difference in gender and years coding. Naturally, there are not as many coders in the 10-20+ year bins. However, we did notice a positive overall difference in female coders in recent years.

Q24: ML Algorithms

We wanted to see what ML algorithms the data scientists that answered this survey used on a regular basis since this relates to what we've learned already in the program. Answers such as logistic regression and neural networks were all answers given and algorithms that we've directly dealt with in the program.

Q12: News

We were curious where data scientists get their news when it comes to data science related topics.We were suprised to find the variance in sources between each year segmentation.

Q20: Data Viz

Data visualization is a big component of data science, so we wanted to find out what data visualization libraries/tools data scientists use. Ggplot2, matplotlib, and seaborn were popular choices and some of which are ones we've used or been exposed to so far.
